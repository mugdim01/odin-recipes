Pizza Recipe

Description

This Homemade Margherita Pizza recipe delivers a classic and delicious pizza experience with a perfectly crisp crust, flavorful tomato sauce, and fresh mozzarella. Topped with fragrant basil leaves and a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil, this pizza is a simple yet satisfying dish that captures the essence of Italian cuisine. Perfect for any pizza night, this recipe brings the taste of an authentic pizzeria right into your home.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water
  • 3 1/2 to 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 can (14 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8 ounces fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in warm water. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine 3 1/2 cups of flour and the salt. Make a well in the center and add the yeast mixture and olive oil.
  3. Mix until a dough forms. If the dough is too sticky, add more flour, a little at a time.
  4. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  5. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1-1.5 hours, or until it has doubled in size.
  6. In a saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  7. Add the crushed tomatoes and oregano. Season with salt and pepper.
  8. Simmer for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove from heat and let it cool.
  9. Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven to preheat as well.
  10. Punch down the risen dough and divide it into two equal parts for two pizzas. Roll each part into a ball.
  11. On a floured surface, roll out each ball of dough into a 12-inch circle.
  12. Transfer the dough to a pizza peel or a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  13. Spread a thin layer of the tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
  14. Arrange the mozzarella slices evenly over the sauce.
  15. Bake in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.
  16. Remove the pizza from the oven and immediately scatter fresh basil leaves on top.
  17. Drizzle with extra virgin olive oil and season with a bit of salt and pepper.
  18. Slice and serve hot.
